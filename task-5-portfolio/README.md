##### &#x20;                               Task 5 Cybercrime Awareness Case Study \& Prevention Guide 





While researching UPI fraud, what surprised me most was the psychological manipulation involved. Scammers don't just use technical "hacks"; they use "social engineering" to create a sense of urgency or excitement that makes even tech-savvy engineering students forget basic safety rules. It was eye-opening to see how a simple request to "receive money" could be flipped into a theft just by exploiting a user's misunderstanding of how QR codes work.



As a result of this research, the one habit I will personally change is enabling biometric locks for my payment apps and strictly never scanning a QR code from an unverified source. As a Digital Ambassador, I now realize that technical knowledge of Python or AI isn't enough; digital literacy must include a constant "pause and reflect" habit before authorizing any digital transaction.

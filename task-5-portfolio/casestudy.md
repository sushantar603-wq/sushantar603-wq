#### Title: The "QR Code" UPI Scam: A Realistic Case Study



Definition: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves deceiving a user into authorizing a transaction that drains their bank account instead of receiving money.



How it Typically Happens:



The Hook: The scammer contacts a student selling an item on a platform like OLX or Facebook Marketplace.



The Bait: The scammer agrees to the full price immediately to build trust and claims they want to pay via UPI.



The Trap: Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a QR code via WhatsApp. They tell the student, "Scan this code to receive the payment in your bank account."



The Execution: When the student scans the code and enters their UPI PIN, they are actually authorizing a "debit" request.



The Result: Money is instantly deducted from the student's account and moved to the scammer's untraceable wallet.



Target Audience: College students and young professionals in India who frequently use digital payment apps for quick transactions.



Consequences: Beyond the immediate financial loss, victims often face significant stress, a sense of violation, and a long administrative process with banks and cyber cells to attempt recovery, which is rarely successful once the money is moved.

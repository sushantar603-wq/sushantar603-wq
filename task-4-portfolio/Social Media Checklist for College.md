Social Media Checklist for College Students



**5 Do's for Responsible Use:**



Do use a professional and clear profile picture on platforms like LinkedIn to create a strong first impression for recruiters.



Do share your academic milestones, such as completing your Python projects or earning HackerRank badges, to build a digital "proof of work."



Do verify the source of any news or technical information before sharing it to prevent the spread of misinformation within your batch.



Do adjust your privacy settings on personal platforms (like Instagram or Facebook) to ensure your private life stays separate from your professional identity.



Do engage respectfully with industry experts and seniors at VIT Bhopal to build a valuable professional network for your future career.



5 Don'ts for Responsible Use:



Don't post offensive, discriminatory, or disrespectful comments that could be traced back to you by future employers during background checks.



Don't share sensitive personal data, such as your university OTP, registration numbers, or home address, on any public platform.



Don't use social media to vent frustrations about professors, specific courses, or the university, as this can permanently damage your professional reputation.



Don't tag colleagues or faculty members in informal or inappropriate photos without their explicit permission.



Don't post content that depicts illegal activities or violates the university's code of conduct, as digital footprints are permanent.

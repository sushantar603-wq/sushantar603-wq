##### &#x20;                                      Task 4 Draft a Professional Email \& Etiquette Guide 







##### A common situation where poor digital communication causes significant issues is the "Reply All" mistake in large organizations. For example, a student might accidentally send a private question about their grades to an entire department email list. This not only causes embarrassment but also clogs the inboxes of hundreds of people and potentially leaks sensitive personal information.



##### To prevent this, the student should have double-checked the "To" field before hitting send. Additionally, using a clear, specific subject line helps the recipient categorize the importance of the email immediately. In professional settings, "thinking before clicking" is the most effective way to maintain a positive digital reputation. By following the etiquette rules practiced in this task, such as using formal salutations and structured bodies, students can avoid these common pitfalls and communicate effectively with professors and recruiters alike.
